# Heat Transfer Critical Radius Analysis

## Project Overview

This project investigates the effect of insulation thickness on heat loss in cylindrical systems and experimentally validates the concept of critical radius of insulation.

The study compares theoretical predictions with experimental observations under natural convection conditions.

---

## Objectives

- Study the effect of insulation thickness on heat loss.
- Experimentally verify the critical radius phenomenon.
- Compare theoretical and experimental heat transfer results.
- Analyze heat transfer under natural convection conditions.

---

## Methodology

- Conducted experiments for multiple insulation radii ranging from 12 mm to 32 mm.
- Measured heat loss under constant electrical heating conditions.
- Compared experimental values with analytical heat transfer equations.
- Validated results using MATLAB calculations.

---

## Results

- Identified the critical insulation radius in the range of approximately 22–25 mm.
- Observed variation in heat loss with increasing insulation thickness.
- Experimental results showed good agreement with theoretical predictions.
